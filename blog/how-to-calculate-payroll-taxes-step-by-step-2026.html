Why Payroll Tax Calculation Is So Complex

A single paycheck might involve federal income tax withholding, Social Security, Medicare, additional Medicare, state income tax, local income tax, FUTA (for the employer), SUTA (for the employer), and sometimes employee SUTA contributions. Each component has different rates, wage caps, and calculation rules.

This guide walks through every component in the order they appear on a paycheck, using a consistent example employee throughout. All rates are current for 2026.

Our example employee: David Kim, married filing jointly, W-4 with Step 2 box unchecked, no dependents, no extra withholding. Annual salary $85,000, paid monthly ($7,083.33/check). California employer. January through December 2026.

Step 1: Determine Gross Wages

Gross wages are what you've agreed to pay before any deductions. For salaried employees, divide the annual salary by pay periods. For hourly employees, multiply hours worked by the wage rate, then add overtime at 1.5× for hours over 40 per week.

💼David's Gross Wages
Annual salary: $85,000 ÷ 12 monthly periods = $7,083.33 gross wages per month
Gross pay this period: $7,083.33

Step 2: Pre-Tax Deductions (Reduce Taxable Wages)

Before calculating any taxes, subtract pre-tax deductions — amounts that reduce the wages subject to federal income tax (and often FICA). Common pre-tax deductions include:

  • 401(k) / 403(b) contributions — reduce federal and state taxable wages (not FICA)
  • Health insurance premiums (employer-sponsored Section 125 cafeteria plan) — reduce both income tax and FICA wages
  • FSA / HSA contributions — reduce both income tax and FICA wages
  • Pre-tax commuter benefits — reduce income tax wages
➖David's Pre-Tax Deductions

401(k) contribution: 6% of gross = $7,083.33 × 6% = $425.00 (reduces income tax wages but not Social Security)

Health insurance: $250/month Section 125 (reduces income tax AND Social Security wages)

Federal income tax wages: $7,083.33 − $425 − $250 = $6,408.33 | FICA wages: $7,083.33 − $250 = $6,833.33

Step 3: Social Security Tax (Employee)

Rate: 6.2% on wages up to $184,500 per year (2026 wage base). Once cumulative wages hit $184,500, Social Security withholding stops for the rest of the year.

🛡️Social Security Calculation
FICA wages: $6,833.33 × 6.2% = $423.67
David earns $85,000/year, well under $184,500, so SS applies all year.
Employee Social Security withheld: $423.67

Step 4: Medicare Tax (Employee)

Rate: 1.45% on all FICA wages — no wage cap. The additional 0.9% kicks in only after cumulative wages exceed $200,000 in the year, so David won't hit that threshold at $85,000/year.

🏥Medicare Calculation
FICA wages: $6,833.33 × 1.45% = $99.08
Employee Medicare withheld: $99.08

Step 5: Federal Income Tax Withholding

This is the most complex piece. Using the Percentage Method from Publication 15-T (2026), with David's 2020 W-4 (MFJ, Step 2 unchecked):

🏛️Federal Income Tax Withholding

Federal income tax wages: $6,408.33 per month

Step 1: Annualize: $6,408.33 × 12 = $76,900

Step 2: No Step 4 adjustments on W-4, so adjusted annual wages = $76,900

Step 3: MFJ Standard Withholding table (Step 2 unchecked). $76,900 falls in 12% bracket ($23,850–$96,950). Tax = $2,385 + 12% × ($76,900 − $23,850) = $2,385 + $6,366 = $8,751 annual

Step 4: Per month: $8,751 ÷ 12 = $729.25

Federal income tax withheld: $729.25 | Effective annual rate: ~10.3% of wages

Step 6: State Income Tax (California Example)

California uses progressive rates from 1% to 13.3%. David earns $6,408.33 in CA taxable wages this period. Using California's 2026 withholding tables (Form DE-4, MFJ):

🌴California State Tax (Example)

Annual CA taxable wages: $76,900. For MFJ, California 2026 withholding table:

2% on $0–$20,824 = $416.48 | 4% on $20,824–$49,368 = $1,141.76 | 6% on $49,368–$63,836 = $868.08 | 8% on $63,836–$76,900 = $1,045.12

Total annual CA withholding ≈ $3,471 ÷ 12 = ~$289.25/month

CA state income tax withheld: ~$289.25/month

Step 7: California SDI (State Disability Insurance)

California requires employees to contribute to the State Disability Insurance (SDI) program. In 2026, the SDI rate is 1.1% with no wage cap (CA removed the wage cap in 2024).

🩺California SDI
FICA wages (CA SDI uses same base): $6,833.33 × 1.1% = $75.17
CA SDI withheld: $75.17/month

Step 8: Post-Tax Deductions

Post-tax deductions are taken from net pay after all taxes are calculated. They don't reduce taxable wages. Examples: Roth 401(k) contributions, garnishments, union dues, after-tax insurance premiums.

Step 9: Net Pay

💰David's Full Monthly Paycheck Summary

Gross wages: $7,083.33

− Pre-tax 401(k): $425.00

− Pre-tax health insurance: $250.00

− Social Security (6.2%): $423.67

− Medicare (1.45%): $99.08

− Federal income tax: $729.25

− CA state income tax: $289.25

− CA SDI (1.1%): $75.17

Net take-home pay: $7,083.33 − $2,291.42 = $4,791.91/month | Effective total withholding rate: ~32.3%

Step 10: Employer Tax Cost

Beyond withholding employee taxes, the employer also pays its own share of FICA plus unemployment taxes. These are additional costs on top of the gross wage:

🏢Employer-Side Taxes (Monthly)

Employer Social Security (6.2% × $6,833.33): $423.67

Employer Medicare (1.45% × $6,833.33): $99.08

FUTA (0.6% × $7,000/12 per month, first 7 months approx.): ~$3.50 avg.

CA SUTA (new employer 3.4%, first $7,000/year): ~$19.83 avg. (first 7 months)

Total monthly employer tax cost beyond gross wages: ~$546 | True annual labor cost for David: $85,000 + ~$6,550 in employer taxes = ~$91,550

Year-End W-2 Reconciliation

At year end, the sum of all David's monthly calculations flows into his W-2:

  • Box 1: Federal wages = Annual gross − pre-tax 401(k) − health insurance = $85,000 − $5,100 − $3,000 = $76,900
  • Box 2: Federal income tax withheld = $729.25 × 12 = $8,751
  • Box 3: Social Security wages = Annual gross − health insurance = $85,000 − $3,000 = $82,000
  • Box 4: Social Security tax withheld = $82,000 × 6.2% = $5,084
  • Box 5: Medicare wages = $82,000 (same as SS wages)
  • Box 6: Medicare tax withheld = $82,000 × 1.45% = $1,189
  • Box 12, Code D: 401(k) contributions = $5,100
  • Boxes 16/17: State wages and state income tax withheld
