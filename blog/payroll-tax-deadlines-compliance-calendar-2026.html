The Payroll Tax Compliance Calendar

Missing a payroll tax deadline is expensive. Federal penalties range from 2% to 15% of the unpaid deposit amount, depending on how late you are. State penalties can be equally harsh. And unlike most business penalties, payroll tax failures can result in personal liability for business owners and responsible parties under the Trust Fund Recovery Penalty.

This calendar covers every federal payroll tax deadline for 2026, including Form 941 quarterly filings, Form 940 FUTA filing, W-2 and W-3 furnishing, and quarterly deposit schedules.

Quick reference: Form 941 due dates: April 30, July 31, Oct 31, Jan 31. Form 940 (FUTA): January 31, 2027. W-2s to employees and SSA: January 31, 2027. 1099-NEC to recipients and IRS: January 31, 2027.

Form 941: Quarterly Employer Tax Return

Form 941 (Employer's Quarterly Federal Tax Return) is the cornerstone of payroll tax compliance. It reports total wages paid, federal income tax withheld, employee and employer FICA taxes, and any adjustments. It's due by the last day of the month following each quarter, unless the employer has a special annual filing status (Form 944 for very small employers).

QuarterPeriod Covered2026 Due Date

10-day extension: If you deposited all taxes due by the due date, you get an automatic additional 10 calendar days to file Form 941. So if you deposit all Q1 taxes by April 30, your Form 941 is due May 10.

Form 940: Annual FUTA Return

Form 940 reports your Federal Unemployment Tax Act (FUTA) liability for the year. Unlike Form 941 (quarterly), Form 940 is filed once per year.

  • 2026 Form 940 due date: January 31, 2027
  • If all FUTA taxes were deposited on time, you get an extension to February 10, 2027
  • FUTA deposits are due quarterly if liability exceeds $500 in a quarter:
QuarterFUTA Deposit Due (if liability > $500)
Q1 2026April 30, 2026
Q2 2026July 31, 2026
Q3 2026October 31, 2026
Q4 2026January 31, 2027 (or with Form 940)

Late Filing and Late Deposit Penalties

Payroll tax penalties are some of the most punishing in the tax code. Here's what to expect:

Deposit 16+ days late10% of unpaid deposit

The Trust Fund Recovery Penalty

The most dangerous payroll tax penalty. If your business fails to deposit withheld employee taxes (income tax + employee FICA), the IRS can assess the Trust Fund Recovery Penalty (TFRP) against any "responsible person" — personally. This means the IRS can pursue you individually even if your business is an LLC or corporation. Responsible persons include owners, officers, payroll managers, or anyone who had authority over the company's finances.

State Payroll Tax Deadlines

State withholding and unemployment tax deadlines vary by state but generally follow these patterns:

  • State income tax withholding: Monthly depositors: 15th of the following month (same as federal). Quarterly filers: last day of the month after the quarter ends.
  • State unemployment tax (SUTA): Quarterly returns typically due April 30, July 31, October 31, January 31 — same as FUTA quarter ends.
  • State W-2 copies: Most states require you to file W-2s with the state by January 31 as well.

New Hire Reporting

All employers must report new hires and rehires to their state's new hire reporting program within 20 days of the hire date (some states require faster reporting). Federal law requires this; states operate their own registries. The information is used to enforce child support orders.

Information you must report: employee name, address, SSN, and hire date; plus employer name, address, and EIN.
