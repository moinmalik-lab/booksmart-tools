Sales Tax Nexus 2026: Economic Nexus Thresholds, State Rules & What Changed

After South Dakota v. Wayfair, out-of-state sellers must collect sales tax in states where they exceed economic nexus thresholds. This guide covers the 2026 thresholds, key changes (Illinois, Maine), marketplace facilitator rules, and how to register.

What Is Sales Tax Nexus?

Sales tax nexus is the connection between your business and a state that legally obligates you to collect and remit sales tax on sales made to customers in that state. Before 2018, nexus required a physical presence — a store, warehouse, office, or employee in the state. The 2018 Supreme Court ruling in South Dakota v. Wayfair, Inc. changed everything.

Today, every state with a sales tax enforces economic nexus — meaning you can be required to collect tax even if you've never set foot in the state, simply because you've crossed a sales threshold there.

2026 Bottom Line: If you sell $100,000+ in goods or services to customers in most states during a year, you likely owe sales tax in those states — even if your business is located elsewhere. Illinois removed its transaction threshold in 2026, joining the majority of states that use revenue-only tests.

Physical Nexus vs. Economic Nexus

Physical Nexus (Traditional)

You have physical nexus in a state if you have:

  • A store, office, or place of business
  • Employees, sales reps, or contractors working in the state
  • Inventory stored in a warehouse or fulfillment center (including Amazon FBA warehouses)
  • Attending trade shows or events in the state for more than a few days per year
  • Leased or owned property in the state

Physical nexus has no threshold — one employee or one box of inventory in a state triggers registration and collection obligations immediately.

Economic Nexus (Post-Wayfair)

Economic nexus is based purely on your economic activity in a state — sales revenue, transaction count, or both. Once you cross the state's threshold, you must register and begin collecting sales tax.

Key 2026 Sales Tax Changes

Illinois Removes Transaction Threshold

Effective January 1, 2026, Illinois eliminated its 200-transaction threshold for remote sellers. Economic nexus in Illinois is now triggered solely by $100,000 in gross receipts from sales into the state. There's an important trap: Illinois counts gross sales including exempt transactions toward the $100,000 limit — unlike most states that count only taxable retail sales. High-volume sellers with many exempt sales need to monitor this carefully.

Digital Products Expanding in Scope

Several states added digital products to their taxable categories in 2026:

  • Maine: Added digital audiovisual services (streaming video) and digital audio services (streaming music) to taxable sales effective 2026. SaaS, streaming, and subscription businesses selling into Maine need to update product taxability classifications.
  • Arkansas: Removed the state-level tax on food (groceries), reducing the standard grocery rate to 0%.

AI-Powered Enforcement in High-Tax States

New York and California now use AI systems to cross-reference marketplace data from platforms like Amazon, Etsy, and Shopify against sellers' filed returns. If you have uncollected sales tax in these states from prior periods, consider using a Voluntary Disclosure Agreement (VDA) to settle — VDAs typically limit the lookback period and waive penalties.

Marketplace Facilitators

If you sell through Amazon, eBay, Etsy, Walmart Marketplace, or other large platforms, the marketplace is generally required to collect and remit sales tax on your behalf in most states. This is known as marketplace facilitator legislation, now in effect in all 45 sales-tax states.

Key implications:

  • Amazon collects and remits sales tax on FBA (Fulfilled by Amazon) orders nationwide
  • Etsy and eBay collect and remit on their marketplace sales
  • If you also sell on your own website (direct channel), those sales are your responsibility separately
  • Marketplace sales typically still count toward your economic nexus thresholds in states where the law includes them
Watch out: Even if Amazon or Etsy handles marketplace sales, if you also sell on your own store (Shopify, BigCommerce, etc.) and your direct sales alone cross a state's threshold, you must register and collect for those direct sales. Marketplace-facilitated sales don't relieve you of nexus obligations from your direct channel.

How to Register for Sales Tax

Once you've determined you have nexus in a state, you must register before you start collecting. Here's the process:

  1. Determine your nexus states — review your sales records by state for the trailing 12 months. Most ecommerce platforms (Shopify, WooCommerce, etc.) can generate this report.
  2. Register with the state tax authority — each state has an online registration portal. Most take 5–15 minutes and issue a permit number within days. The Streamlined Sales Tax (SST) program covers 24 member states with simplified registration.
  3. Configure your storefront to collect the correct rate for each state/county/city
  4. Begin collecting on the first sale after your registration date (nexus is not retroactive from the collection standpoint, though you may owe back taxes)
  5. File returns according to each state's schedule — monthly, quarterly, or annually depending on your volume

Common Sales Tax Exemptions

Sales tax exemptions vary enormously by state. Common categories include:

  • Groceries: Most states exempt unprepared food. Arkansas eliminated its state food tax in 2026. A few states (like Mississippi) tax groceries at a reduced rate.
  • Prescription drugs: Exempt in nearly all states
  • Medical devices: Largely exempt, but varies by item
  • Manufacturing machinery: Most states exempt machinery and equipment used in production
  • Resale certificates: Goods purchased for resale are exempt if the buyer provides a valid resale certificate
  • Services: Most states tax tangible personal property but vary widely on services (haircuts, accounting, legal, software)
