How to Choose Bookkeeping Software in 2026

The bookkeeping software market has consolidated significantly. In 2026, most small businesses will find their answer in one of five platforms: QuickBooks Online, Xero, FreshBooks, Wave, or Zoho Books. Each occupies a clear niche. The worst mistake is choosing based on a generic feature checklist rather than the specific way your business actually operates.

Before reviewing any software, answer these three questions: (1) How many people need access? (2) Do you sell physical products? (3) Do you have employees who need payroll? Your answers will eliminate two or three options immediately.

At a glance: QuickBooks Online = best overall for US small business. Xero = best for multi-user teams. FreshBooks = best for freelancers and service businesses. Wave = best free option. Zoho Books = best value if already in the Zoho ecosystem.

QuickBooks Online — Best Overall for US Small Business

QuickBooks Online is the market leader for good reasons. It's the accounting software that your US accountant, tax preparer, and bookkeeper almost certainly use. The depth of US-specific features — payroll, 1099 management, sales tax, and reporting — is unmatched by any competitor.

Plans and Pricing

  • Solopreneur: ~$20/mo — one-person businesses, basic income/expense tracking
  • Simple Start: ~$38/mo — 1 user, full accounting, invoicing
  • Essentials: ~$75/mo — 3 users, adds bill management
  • Plus: ~$115/mo — 5 users, adds inventory and project tracking (most popular)
  • Advanced: ~$275/mo — 25 users, custom workflows, batch processing

Best For

  • Retail, e-commerce, and product-based businesses needing inventory tracking
  • Businesses with employees who want payroll fully integrated with their books
  • Any business whose accountant works primarily in QuickBooks (most US CPAs)
  • Growing businesses that need strong reporting and scalability

Limitations

  • Price increases of 12–17% annually make long-term costs hard to predict
  • User limits by tier — 5-user cap on Plus can feel restrictive for growing teams
  • Can feel overwhelming for very simple use cases (freelancers with basic needs)
The accountant argument: This is QBO's strongest card. With 400,000+ QuickBooks ProAdvisors in the US, finding a certified bookkeeper or CPA who works in QBO is trivial. Sharing your books at tax time takes one invitation email. Every other platform requires your accountant to learn a new system.

Xero — Best for Multi-User Teams and International Business

Xero's unlimited-user pricing model is its single biggest differentiator. While QuickBooks charges more as your team grows, Xero's $37/month Standard plan covers unlimited users. For businesses where multiple people need access to the books — owners, bookkeepers, office managers, accountants — Xero can save hundreds to thousands of dollars annually.

Plans and Pricing

  • Starter: ~$20/mo — unlimited users, but limited to 20 invoices and 5 bills per month
  • Standard: ~$37/mo — unlimited users, unlimited invoicing, bulk reconciliation
  • Premium: ~$78/mo — adds multi-currency, expense claims, project tracking

Best For

  • Teams of 3+ people who all need regular access to the books
  • Businesses with international operations or multi-currency transactions
  • Tech-savvy businesses that rely heavily on third-party app integrations (1,000+ in Xero's marketplace)
  • Businesses that value a clean, modern interface and are willing to learn a non-QBO workflow

Limitations

  • No native US payroll — requires Gusto integration (works well, but separate subscription and support)
  • Fewer US-based accountants and bookkeepers work in Xero vs. QBO — harder to find local help
  • Starter plan's invoice and bill limits are genuinely restrictive — most businesses need Standard

FreshBooks — Best for Freelancers and Service Businesses

FreshBooks was built for a specific user: the service professional who bills clients, tracks time, and wants the simplest possible bookkeeping. It's not trying to be QuickBooks — it prioritizes ease of use and beautiful invoicing over accounting depth.

Plans and Pricing

  • Lite: ~$19/mo — 5 billable clients, basic invoicing, expense tracking
  • Plus: ~$33/mo — 50 billable clients, proposals, recurring invoices
  • Premium: ~$60/mo — unlimited clients, project profitability
  • Select: Custom pricing — two users included, dedicated account manager

Extra users cost $11/month each on all plans — FreshBooks gets expensive for teams quickly.

Best For

  • Freelancers, consultants, and solo professionals who primarily need invoicing and time tracking
  • Small service businesses (lawyers, designers, photographers, coaches) billing clients by project or hour
  • Business owners who want simple bookkeeping without an accounting background

Limitations

  • Not suitable for product-based businesses (limited inventory, no COGS tracking)
  • Bank reconciliation only available on Plus plan and up
  • Extra user costs make it expensive for teams of 3+
  • Fewer integrations than QBO or Xero

Wave — Best Free Option

Wave is the only major accounting platform with a genuinely free core product. For very small businesses and solopreneurs with simple needs, Wave offers real accounting software — not just a spreadsheet — at zero cost for the base features.

Pricing

  • Free plan: Core accounting, unlimited invoicing, expense tracking, basic reports
  • Wave Pro: ~$16/mo — auto-imports bank transactions, receipt capture, better support
  • Payments: 2.9% + $0.60 per card transaction, 1% + $0.00 for ACH (paid separately)
  • Wave Payroll: $40/mo base (tax service states) + $6/employee — separate add-on

Best For

  • Solo entrepreneurs, side-business owners, and very small businesses with basic bookkeeping needs
  • Business owners who need invoicing but can't justify a monthly software fee
  • Early-stage startups tracking basic income and expenses before the business has real complexity

Limitations

  • Limited integrations — Wave doesn't connect to most third-party business apps
  • Basic reporting — sufficient for simple books, inadequate for complex businesses
  • Customer support is minimal on the free plan
  • Payroll only available in some US states (check availability for your state)
  • Not designed to scale — when your business grows, you'll likely migrate to QBO or Xero
Wave's business model: Wave is free because Intuit acquired it and monetizes through payment processing fees and payroll subscriptions. The free accounting features are a funnel to those paid products. If you process a lot of card payments, the 2.9% rate is standard but adds up. Compare processing fees before committing to Wave Payments.

Zoho Books — Best for the Zoho Ecosystem

Zoho Books is a full-featured accounting platform that makes most sense if your business already uses other Zoho products — Zoho CRM, Zoho Inventory, Zoho Projects, or Zoho Payroll. The native integration across the Zoho ecosystem is genuinely powerful. As a standalone accounting tool, it's capable but lacks the US-specific depth of QBO and the user-community of Xero.

Plans and Pricing

  • Free plan: For businesses with revenue under $50K/year — basic invoicing, client portal
  • Standard: ~$15/mo — 3 users, 5,000 invoices/year, automated workflows
  • Professional: ~$40/mo — 5 users, full purchase orders, inventory
  • Premium: ~$60/mo — 10 users, advanced reporting, budgeting
  • Elite: ~$120/mo — 10 users, advanced inventory, warehouse management

Best For

  • Businesses already using Zoho CRM, Zoho Inventory, or other Zoho tools — the native sync is a major advantage
  • Cost-conscious businesses that need more features than Wave but less than QBO Plus
  • Businesses with international customers — strong multi-currency and tax compliance

Limitations

  • Very few US accountants and CPAs work in Zoho Books — finding bookkeeping help is harder
  • Payroll requires Zoho Payroll (separate subscription, limited US state coverage)
  • Integrations outside the Zoho ecosystem are more limited than QBO or Xero

Honorable Mentions

Sage Business Cloud Accounting

Sage is a century-old accounting software company that has transitioned to cloud. Sage Business Cloud is strong for compliance-heavy businesses and nonprofits. Starting around $10–$25/month, it's competitive on price and has solid audit trails. Widely used in the UK; less common in the US market.

Bench (Bookkeeping Service, Not Software)

Bench is a "bookkeeping as a service" product — you connect your accounts, and a team of bookkeepers does your monthly books. Starting around $299/month, it's expensive but eliminates the need to do bookkeeping yourself. Best for business owners who want zero involvement in their own books and have the budget for it.

QuickBooks Desktop Enterprise

For manufacturing, wholesale, and large contractor businesses that have outgrown QBO, Desktop Enterprise ($1,922+/year) remains the most powerful US accounting desktop platform. Industry-specific editions, 40-user capacity, and advanced inventory make it the right call for certain business types.
