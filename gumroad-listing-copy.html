How to Set Up on Gumroad — Step by Step

  1. 1
    Go to gumroad.com and create a free seller account. Use your professional email. Add a simple profile photo (or your logo) and a short bio: "Bookkeeper working with US small businesses. Building free tools to save you time."
  2. 2
    Click New Product → Digital Product. Upload your HTML file(s) as a ZIP archive. Give the product the name and description from the listing copy above.
  3. 3
    Set your price. For the free tool: set to $0 with "pay what you want" enabled, suggested price $5. For the bundle: set to $19 flat.
  4. 4
    Add a cover image. Use Canva (free) — create a simple 1280×720px image with the tool name, your brand colors, and a short tagline. Dark background, white text looks professional.
  5. 5
    Enable the email collection checkbox. This is essential — it lets you email buyers later when you launch new tools.
  6. 6
    Publish the product and copy the Gumroad link. Add it to your landing page, blog posts, and social media.
  7. 7
    Set up a thank-you email in Gumroad (Product → Emails → Receipt email). Personalize it — don't use the default. See the email template below.

Email 2 — Day 3 Subject: The 3 invoice mistakes that cost small businesses thousands
Hi [First Name], Quick one today. After years of working with US small business clients, these are the three invoice mistakes I see most often: 1. No late fee policy on the invoice. Most businesses only think about late fees when an invoice is already 60 days overdue. By then, adding a fee is awkward. Put your terms on every invoice from day one — even if you never need to charge the fee. 2. Net 30 when the client's payable cycle is Net 45. If your client only processes vendor payments twice a month, sending Net 30 invoices just means they'll pay you one cycle late, every time. Ask new clients how they process invoices before you set your terms. 3. Not following up at day 7. Most late payments aren't intentional — they're forgotten. A polite email at 7 days overdue ("Just checking in on invoice #1234") resolves the majority of late payments before they become a problem. More soon. — [Your Name] P.S.
