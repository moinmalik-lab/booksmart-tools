Meals Tax Guide — States With Separate or Higher Restaurant Tax Rates
Most states apply their standard sales tax to restaurant meals. These states charge extra meals taxes or have notable differences. Source: State revenue departments, Tax Foundation 2025.

Virginia County-level

State sales tax on prepared food: 5.3% (4.3% state + 1% mandatory local). Counties and cities may add their own meals tax on top — up to 6% for cities, 4% for counties.

  • Arlington County (from Jul 2025)5% + 5.3% = 10.3%
  • Fairfax County (from Jan 2026)4% + 5.3% = 9.3%
  • Alexandria City4% + 5.3% = 9.3%
  • Richmond City7.5% + 5.3% = 12.8%
  • Virginia Beach5.5% + 5.3% = 10.8%
  • Roanoke City6.5% + 5.3% = 11.8%
  • No local tax counties5.3% total

Massachusetts City/Town option

State sales tax on meals: 6.25%. All cities and towns may add a local meals excise tax of up to 0.75%. Most major cities have adopted the full rate.

  • Boston (local 0.75%)7.00% total
  • Cambridge (local 0.75%)7.00% total
  • Worcester (local 0.75%)7.00% total
  • Springfield (local 0.75%)7.00% total
  • Towns with no local tax6.25% total

New Hampshire State-level only

No general sales tax. Instead, NH charges a statewide 8.5% Meals & Rooms Tax on all prepared food and restaurant meals. No local additions permitted.

  • All cities and towns8.5% flat

Note: The rate was reduced from 9% to 8.5% effective June 2021 and remains at 8.5% through 2025.

Vermont State + local option

State meals & rooms tax: 9% on restaurant meals (higher than the 6% general sales tax). Select towns may also add a local option tax of 1%.

  • Burlington (local 1%)10% total
  • Stowe (local 1%)10% total
  • Montpelier (local 1%)10% total
  • No local tax towns9% total

Maine State-level only

Meals tax: 8% on restaurant food and prepared food (higher than the 5.5% general sales tax). The 8% rate applies statewide with no local additions.

  • All cities and towns8% flat

Washington D.C. City-wide

Restaurant meals are taxed at 10% in D.C. (vs 6% on general goods). This higher rate applies to all restaurant and prepared food purchases.

  • All of Washington D.C.10% flat

Connecticut State-wide surcharge

General sales tax: 6.35%. Meals tax: 7.35% — a 1% surcharge applies to all restaurant meals and certain prepared food statewide. No local additions.

  • All cities and towns7.35% flat

Tennessee Reduced grocery rate

Restaurant meals taxed at full 7% state rate (plus local avg ~2.55%). Grocery items get reduced 4% state rate. One of highest combined meals tax states.

  • Nashville/Davidson Co.9.25% avg combined
  • Memphis/Shelby Co.9.75% avg combined
  • State rate only7.00%

States where standard sales tax applies to meals (no extra meals tax)

Most states simply apply their regular sales tax rate to restaurant meals. These include: Alabama, Arizona, Arkansas, California, Colorado, Florida, Georgia, Idaho, Illinois, Indiana, Iowa, Kansas, Kentucky, Louisiana, Maryland, Michigan, Minnesota, Mississippi, Missouri, Nebraska, Nevada, New Jersey, New Mexico, New York, North Carolina, North Dakota, Ohio, Oklahoma, Pennsylvania, Rhode Island, South Carolina, South Dakota, Texas, Utah, Virginia*, Washington, West Virginia, Wisconsin, Wyoming.

*Virginia's standard rate already includes a mandatory 1% local add-on. Counties/cities may add further meals taxes on top.

States with NO sales tax on meals: Alaska, Delaware, Montana, Oregon. Note: NH has no sales tax but imposes a specific 8.5% meals & rooms tax.
